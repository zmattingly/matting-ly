Bio

Born in New York City, raised in Salt Lake City, geek to the bone.

Ever since getting my grubby little hands on an NES controller at the age of 4, I've been obsessed with computers. Sometimes to my parent's chagrin.

They finally broke down when I was 7 and bought me a Gameboy, the original brick version that was hefty enough to break a window. Tetris became my life, I'd see blocks falling whenever I closed my eyes.

From there I graduated onto a old Apple II machine a family friend gifted to us when I was 8 and not long after that a 486 PC. They were clunky, but I loved them and loved to see what I could tweak and change about them.

When I was 13, the original Half-Life was released and changed my life forever. The game came bundled with a powerful 3D Map Editor and this soon came to dominate my free time. I spent hours upon hours creating custom maps for Deathmatch and Team Fortress Classic.

Through High School my love affair with the world of computing continued, spending my free time learning basic scripts, navigating FTP sites and learning HTML/Javascript.

My career as a developer has not been a traditional one, I'm entirely self-taught but have had great professional mentors and I look forward every day to learning new skills and sharpening existing ones.
